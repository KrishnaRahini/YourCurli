Alleppey Beach

Alappuzha (or Alleppey) is a city on the Laccadive Sea in the southern Indian state of Kerala. It's best known for houseboat cruises along the rustic Kerala backwaters, a network of tranquil canals and lagoons. Alappuzha Beach is the site of the 19th-century Alappuzha Lighthouse.

Open time:5 am, Close time:7 pm

Mattancherry Palace

This Portuguese palace, also known as the Dutch Palace is one of the prime places to visit in Kochi due to its historic relevance. This quadrangular palace built in typical Kerala Nalukettu style has a courtyard in the centre where is seated the Pazhayannur Bhagavati' the goddess of Kochi royal family..

Open time:9 am, Close time:7 pm

Eravikulam National Park

This national park was the once the hunting place of the British. Today this park protects wild animals at its best. This national park is situated at an elevation of 7000 feet above the sea level. This sanctuary is famous Nilgiri tahr, a highly endangered wild goat. This is the proud of Munnar.

Open time:9 am, Close time:6 pm

Mahabaleshwar

Owing to its glorious past, the hill station by the name of Mahabaleshwar has become quite the hot spot for tourists each year during summers. Surrounded by the sky-scraping mountains ranges of Western Ghats, this little hill station is nothing short of spectacular with its picturesque views, lush green forests, and sparkling rivers.

Sikkim

The best thing about a place as mystifying as Sikkim is the fact that even though it is inexplicably beautiful, yet it is one of the most unexplored places in India. Topping the list of tourist places in India during summer, this place has unexplored caves, gushing rapids, cascading waterfalls and astounding trekking trails.

Dharamshala

Set up in a quaint setting, the hill station of Dharamshala is among one of the best places to stay in Himachal Pradesh. Attracting tourists from around the world, this little place is nothing less than ideal for people seeking spiritual enhancement. There a number of places to visit in Dharamshala.
